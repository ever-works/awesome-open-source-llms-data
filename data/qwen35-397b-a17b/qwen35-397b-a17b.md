## Overview
Qwen3.5-397B-A17B is the latest flagship model from the Qwen series, known for its large Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture and advanced multimodal reasoning capabilities.

## Features
- **State-of-the-art performance:** Exhibits strong capabilities in instruction following, reasoning, coding, agentic, and multilingual tasks, competitive with frontier closed-source models.
- **Ultra-long context:** Supports a 262K token native context window, extendable up to over 1 million tokens, ideal for AI agents, RAG, and long-term conversations.
- **Multimodal reasoning:** Integrates vision and language early in its architecture, allowing it to reason across text, images, video, and documents within a unified framework.
- **Tool calling:** Capable of calling tools such as code interpreters and image search during multimodal reasoning.
- **Global language coverage:** Expands multilingual coverage to over 200 languages and dialects.
- **High decoding throughput:** Delivers 8.6x–19x higher decoding throughput compared to earlier generations, improving serving efficiency for large-scale deployments.

## Model Family
The Qwen3.5 family includes a range of models:
- **Medium model series (e.g., Qwen3.5-35B-A3B, Qwen3.5-122B-A10B, Qwen3.5-27B):** Focuses on improving capability without simply increasing parameter counts, surpassing larger older models through architectural improvements, higher-quality data, and scaled reinforcement learning.
- **Small models (0.8B, 2B, 4B, and 9B):** Designed for resource-constrained environments, supporting lightweight deployments such as edge inference, compact agents, and mobile applications, while retaining multimodal capabilities and improved RL training.

## Resource Considerations
Running long sequences can require around 1 TB of GPU memory (accounting for model weights, KV cache, and activation memory). Users may need to reduce context length if OOM errors occur, while keeping at least 128K tokens to preserve reasoning performance.
