## Overview

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) was introduced by Google in 2018. Unlike generative LLMs, BERT is a transformer-based model that processes sequences of data to produce contextual embeddings.

## Architecture

- Stack of transformer encoders
- 342 million parameters
- Pre-trained on a large corpus of text
- Bidirectional context understanding (processes text left-to-right and right-to-left simultaneously)

## Key Features

- Contextual embeddings for downstream tasks
- Fine-tuning for specific NLP tasks
- Natural language inference
- Sentence text similarity
- Improved query understanding in Google Search (2019)

## Use Cases

- Text classification
- Named entity recognition
- Question answering
- Sentence similarity
- Search query understanding

## Legacy

BERT remains widely used in research and production systems, though it has been superseded by more advanced generative transformer models for many tasks.
